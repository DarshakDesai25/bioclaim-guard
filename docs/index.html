The problem

AI generates answers.
BioClaim Guard verifies them.

Large language models produce medical claims that sound authoritative but may be overgeneralized, outdated, or simply wrong.

⚠ Silent failures in medical AI

Overgeneralization — applying specific study findings to all patients
Causation vs correlation — presenting associations as proven causes
Animal/in-vitro studies presented as human clinical evidence
Outdated findings contradicted by newer research
Effect size exaggeration — inflating magnitude of benefits or risks

Claim: "Aspirin prevents heart attacks in all adults."

⚠
Misleading / Overgeneralized

Claim: "Vitamin C cures the common cold."

✗
Contradicted
Cochrane reviews (Hemilä & Chalker, 2013, 2023) synthesizing over 9,000 participants found that vitamin C supplementation does not prevent colds in the general population, nor does it "cure" them. Technical design

How it works

BioClaim Guard implements two core components: Retrieval-Augmented Generation and structured Prompt Engineering.

1

PICO extraction

Claude decomposes the input claim into Population, Intervention, Comparison, and Outcome — the gold standard framework in evidence-based medicine.

2

PubMed RAG

The PICO components drive a targeted query to NCBI E-utilities, retrieving the top-k most relevant peer-reviewed studies from PubMed's 37M+ citations.

3

Evidence alignment

Claude compares the claim against the retrieved studies using structured verification prompts that check for all known failure modes: overgeneralization, causation errors, and more.

4

Structured verdict

The system outputs a classification (Supported / Misleading / Contradicted / Insufficient), confidence level, evidence quality rating, and specific issues found.

5

Cited sources

Every verdict links to the original PubMed studies, enabling users to trace conclusions back to primary evidence and verify independently.

6

Transparent uncertainty

When evidence is absent or weak, the system says so — never fabricating certainty. Confidence levels and evidence quality are explicit in every response.

Ethical framework

Responsible AI in healthcare

🔒

No personal data

BioClaim Guard uses only publicly available, de-identified PubMed data. No user or patient data is collected or stored.

🔗

Full transparency

Every verdict includes citations linking directly to original PubMed studies. All reasoning is traceable.

⚖

Bias awareness

Clinical trials often underrepresent certain demographics. The system explicitly flags population mismatches in its PICO analysis.

📊

Uncertainty first

When evidence is weak or absent, the system classifies claims as "Insufficient Evidence" rather than fabricating confidence.

🚫

Not medical advice

BioClaim Guard is a research and education tool. Every response includes a clear disclaimer to consult a healthcare professional.

📚

Open source

All code is publicly available on GitHub for inspection, reproducibility, and community improvement.
